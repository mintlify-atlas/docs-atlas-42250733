FieldStation42 transforms your Linux computer or Raspberry Pi into a fully functioning broadcast and cable TV simulator. Instead of choosing something to watch, you flip channels — and scheduled programming plays on its own continuous timeline, complete with commercials, station bumps, and all the in-between content that made old-school TV feel alive.

## How it works

FieldStation42 manages your media library as a real TV schedule. The system moves through four stages each time you set up a channel:

1. **Media library** — you organize your video files into directories that FieldStation42 can scan.
2. **Catalog** — FieldStation42 indexes your media, detecting file duration, chapters, and commercial break points.
3. **Schedule** — the scheduler builds a continuous daily lineup from your catalog, filling slots with the right content at the right time and inserting commercials in the gaps.
4. **Playback** — the field player reads the live schedule and hands off to MPV at the correct offset, so channels always play in real time regardless of when you tune in.

Every channel runs on its own independent timeline. Tuning to a channel mid-show drops you in at the correct point, just as if you had a real cable box.

## System requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Operating system | Linux — Raspberry Pi OS Bookworm recommended, Ubuntu, Mint, Debian, or WSL on Windows |
| Python | 3.10 or newer |
| Media player | MPV |

<Note>
  FieldStation42 runs on standard desktop Linux as well as Raspberry Pi hardware. Windows users can run it under WSL (Windows Subsystem for Linux).
</Note>
